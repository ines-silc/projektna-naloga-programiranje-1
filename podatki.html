A Guide To Peaceful Trip To Slovenia!

Slovenia, the beautiful country in central Europe is a prime attraction among the tourists. With its rich scenery of mountains and rivers, it exudes a charm that captivates every wander-loving mind. Not for the natural splendor only, this European country has also conquered the heart of the travelers with its extremely delicious cuisine. If you are planning to have a tryst with this Adriatic land, then get an eyeful of the laws and customs as well as the safety and security rules that you need to abide by in Slovenia.

Safety And Security:

Exercise common safety measures that can ensure the security of your belongings. Be alert, especially near the jam packed tourist areas where pickpockets and thieves loiter around in search for a target. If you are unaware of their existence, they will snatch your bag or steal your valuables which may create a lot of complications for you. Stay away from any financial dealings as they can be a part of scam. Do not respond to the claims of tax refunds as it may rob you of your wealth. When driving on the road, make sure that you have vignettes as this is a custom in Slovenia. Police may charge heavy fine if you fail to show any vignette to them as it implies breaching the law. In cold seasons, mainly from 15th November up to 15th March, winter equipments are a must for your vehicle. You should keep you headlights on even during day time and must carry a safety triangle, first aid box and a high visibility jacket in your car. Traffic laws are quite stringent in Slovenia, so never break them or you have to pay a lump sum fine.Before setting out for hiking and mountaineering, know about the weather conditions. Snow slides and avalanches are quite common that may disrupt your trip and can also make you injured.

Local Laws and Customs:

When in Slovenia, you must carry your passport or a copy of it whenever you are roaming around the streets. You must be registered with the police within three days of your entrance in Slovenia. Jaywalkers may be heavily fined by the patrolling cops.

Health:

Follow the advice of your general practitioner about the vaccination 6-8 months prior to your grand Slovenian trip. Check whether you have travel insurances that cover the cost of medical treatments out of the country. Make sure that your insurance company also offers repatriation, if necessary. Carry enough medicines if you have any chronic illness, so that there is no shortage when you are on trip. If any emergency medical assistance is required, just dial 112 and get in touch with the emergency services.

Natural Disasters:

If you are about to visit the western part of Slovenia, then be aware as this place is prone to frequent earthquakes and tremors can be felt quite often. So, take advice of the local authorities if you feel light seismic activities.

Money:

Euro is the national currency of Slovenia. You can find ATMs and exchange banks at a large number. Credit and debit cards are also accepted natiowide.
